## Design Context

### Users
Cross-border e-commerce shoppers in **Europe, North America, and Southeast Asia** browsing a branded storefront for **Halloween-themed ceramic décor**, especially **hyper-realistic ceramic pumpkins**. Context: discovery and trust-building on a marketing landing page before category pages and checkout.

### Brand Personality
**Artisan, spooky-elegant, collectible** — premium ceramics that feel like heirloom Halloween props, not mass-market plastic. Voice: warm, slightly theatrical, confident in craft.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Halloween-native UI**: deep midnight/violet-black bases, ember orange and toxic green accents, subtle grain and vignette. **Not** generic purple-gradient SaaS; **not** childish clip-art. Flat UI icons for utility (lang, cart, account). **High-motion** allowed: intro spectacle, scroll reveals, hover depth — respect `prefers-reduced-motion` where feasible.

### Design Principles
1. **Hero-first**: full-viewport banner with clear title, slogan, and a **3D rotating pumpkin** as the product signature.
2. **Symmetrical nav breathing room**: logo and icon clusters inset from viewport edges (horizontal padding), nav links visually centered.
3. **Separation of IA**: primary nav targets **separate routes** (products, resources, about); landing body does not duplicate that navigation as a footer menu block.
4. **Social proof with imagery**: reviews emphasize **large, zoomable photos**.
5. **Clear conversion path**: dedicated CTA strip toward **account / login**.
